Recalling that for SRs the nominal distances to be successful on a 1st down is 5 yards and on 2nd down is 3.5 yards. Given this our median distances aren't looking good. The median distances were 8.5 yards for 2nd down and 5.0 yards for 3rd down versus the median gained of 4.5 yards on 2nd and 1.0 yards on 3rd. The one good bit of data from the Yards to Success vs Yards Gained by Down is that on 3rd and short we were successful on 5 out of 6 tries.



I did make some box and whisker plots for the Distance to 1st/TD and Yards Gained both by down. For the Distance to 1st/TD graph first down is going to be very unbox like and centered on 10 yards unless the game is just some kind of wild wrt penalties on 1st down. On 2nd down 50% of our plays were from 5.5 to 10 yards from a 1st/TD. On 3rd down 50% of our plays were from 1.5 to 9.5 yards from a 1st/TD.

Looking at the Yards Gained box-and-whisker plot, 1st down has 50% of our yards gained from 0 to just under 6 yards. On 2nd down 50% of the yards gained is from 0 to just over 12 yards with a median of 4.5, but with 50% of the distance being from 5.5 to 10 yards, median of 8.5 yards that just doesn't feel good.





The TL;DR of this is that I have no historic perspective, but it looks not good.
