What else can we look at? Well since I did decide to start calculating XR I guess we might as well go ahead and look at that. They had a mean XR of 0.132 (stdev = 0.060) and against us that went up to 0.153 which is 0.351 standard deviations above their mean. That doesn't seem to be that much of jump especially given that they had some really low XRs strewn about their schedule: 0.091 Bowling Green, 0.093 Penn State, 0.030 Maryland, and 0.016 Iowa. They did have 5 games with a higher XR than vs us: 0.171 Purdue, 0.177 East Carolina, 0.193 Minnesota, 0.194 Michigan State, and 0.217 UNLV. And it was almost the exact same as they had against Ohio St: 0.150.

Our season average XR was 0.147 (stdev = 0.030) and we had an XR of 0.091. That is -1.873 standard deviations below our mean! And that is what I (amongst many others) would say was what lost us this game, we couldn't manufacture the boom plays and were stuck with lots of busts.
