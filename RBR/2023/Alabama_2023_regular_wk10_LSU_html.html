While I still am not sure how to interpret the mean SR by Drive, μ_SR, I know that a μ_SR(game) = 0.567 is good especially when compared to μ_SR(season) = 0.366. As I mentioned in a comment to balloons article, https://www.rollbamaroll.com/2023/10/23/23928199/graphing-the-tide-vs-tennessee-second-halves-and-red-zones-yet-again?_gl=1*1gprbbn*#comments, maybe it is a measure of consistency across the game when compared to the game SR. If so then comparing μ_SR(game) = 0.567 to our SR = 0.536 looks pretty good.

For comparison, LSU's μ_SR(game) = 0.5 and SR = 0.509. I continued to feel like they were a major threat until Daniels was unfortunately injured on what looked like a legal tackle to me. Overall we had a few lower SR drives and a few higher SR drives and they seemed to be pretty consistent as seen in the table below. Playing with the numbers a bit we had a differential of μ_SR(game) - SR = 0.567 - 0.536 = 0.031 and they had a differential of μ_SR(game) - SR = 0.500 - 0.509 = -0.009. Looking at their past games it is interesting that their lowest mean SR by Drive was against Missouri which they beat and their highest SR was against Ole Miss who beat them. I've not had time to watch them this year so I don't know what to make of these numbers.

Looking at the SR by Distance to 1st/TD facted by downs below, second down looks like what you would expect to happen as you get farther from you goal, decreasing success rates. But on third down we have a high SR from 1-3 yards out and then after dropping from 1.000 to 0.500 our SR increases the further we were from a 1st/TD back to 1.000 in the 10-12 yard distance.

Interestingly... or not, all of LSU's touchdowns came on first down plays. Two were in the 1-3 yard range, one in the 7-9 yard range, and one in the 10-12 yard range.
