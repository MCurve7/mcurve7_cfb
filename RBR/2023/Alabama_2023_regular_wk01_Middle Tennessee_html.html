While responding to RaulTyde's comment below it reminded me of how I was interpreting my Yards to Successful vs Yards gained by Down graph. So I decided to generate a table that represents what I look at. I think the Down column is self explanatory. The Count column is the number of that down played. The column Δ_plays is the change in the number of plays played in consecutive downs. I think the mean, standard deviation (std), and median of the distance and gained are self explanatory. (I can add a column for median gained if anyone wants it... which I might add whether it's asked for or not :-P).

What I look for is for Δ_plays to have a nice decrease from 1st to 2nd to 3rd downs (and hope there were no turnovers making the numbers look good). I also look to see if the mean/median distance is decreasing by down. Obviously a large mean gained is desired.

For the SR by Drive graph I added a game average line (dotdash) and a season average line (dashed). I find Success Rate of most interest in terms of how well it determines if a drive ends in a TD or not. We had 13 drives in this game, 8 of which resulted in a TD. Drive number 7 is a throwaway drive since it was the last drive before the half, we got the ball with 53 seconds left in the half, at the 12 yard line, and were winning 28-0. With that said, every drive with a SR at .5 or higher scored a TD. One of the three drives with a SR = 1/3 resulted in a TD.



In terms of number of plays per drive, we ranged from 1 up to 11. We had five drives of 6 plays (3 TDs, 3/5), three of 2 plays (3 TDs, 2/3), two of 3 plays (0 TDs, 0/2), one of 1 play (1 TD, 1/1), one of 10 plays (1 TD, 1/1), and one of 11 plays (1 TD, 1/1). I've never really paid attention to this part of the data, but since they say we are going back to more Joyless Murderball it might be interesting to keep track of.
